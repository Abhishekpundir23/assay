The gap

Why your AI passes review,
then breaks in front of customers

The demo is the easy 80%. You typed three reasonable questions, got three good answers, and shipped. Then production happens:

  • A customer pastes an article into the chat and the model follows instructions hidden inside it.
  • A benign question gets refused — or something that should escalate gets answered anyway.
  • The RAG answer sounds confident and cites a policy that doesn't exist.
  • You swap to a cheaper model, every spot-check passes — and a regression you can't see quietly degrades a quarter of your answers.

"It works when I try it" is not a quality signal — it's the absence of one.

The method

Three steps. One scorecard.

A documented method run the same way every time — so results are comparable across runs, versions, and vendors.

STEP 01

Map the failure surface

I map your feature to a curated failure-mode taxonomy and design a test suite for the modes that matter to your product — not a generic benchmark.

STEP 02

Run, red-team & score

Two layers of scoring: deterministic checks (leaks? valid JSON? PII? latency?) plus an LLM-as-judge for the judgment calls — including adversarial injection and jailbreak cases.

STEP 03

Deliver scorecard + fixes

An overall grade, a pass rate per failure mode, the exact failing cases with input/output/why, and a prioritized list of what to fix first.

Optional retainer: I re-run your frozen suite on every model or prompt change — so a swap that silently breaks things gets caught before it ships.

FAQ

Questions

What exactly do you audit?
Any LLM-powered feature — a chatbot, a RAG / knowledge assistant, a tool-using agent, or a backend model. I can test a hosted HTTP endpoint, a function in your codebase, or a model directly.
Do I have to give you production access or data?
No production write access, ever. For the free mini-audit I work against your public or staging endpoint with synthetic and adversarial inputs I generate — no customer data required.
Isn't an LLM judging another LLM unreliable?
It is — if you don't check it. I use deterministic checks wherever a question has a hard answer, reserve the LLM judge for genuine judgment calls, and report how much that judge agrees with human labels (Cohen's κ). A judge nobody validated is worthless; a validated one is leverage.
How is this different from the evals my team already runs?
Most in-house evals are a happy-path set written by the people who built the feature, scored by "looks good to me." Assay brings an outside taxonomy, adversarial cases, a measured judge, and regression monitoring across versions. The difference between checking your own homework and an audit.
How long does it take?
The free mini-audit turns around in a few days. A full fixed-scope audit is typically 1–2 weeks depending on failure-mode count and red-team depth — with price and scope agreed before I start.
